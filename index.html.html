    📋 How to report a concern safely

    Before reporting, consider your safety first. You do not have to report to police — you can report to an NGO, labor inspector, or trusted community organization. You have the right to report anonymously in many jurisdictions.
    🔒
    You can report anonymously

    Many hotlines and NGOs accept anonymous reports. You do not have to identify yourself to raise a concern.

    📸
    Preserve evidence safely

    Screenshot messages, save documents, note dates. You do not need to confront anyone to preserve evidence.

    🤝
    Report to an NGO first

    If unsure about involving police, contacting a local NGO first is often safer and helps you understand your options.

    🌐
    Report to the platform

    Report suspicious job postings directly to the platform where you found them.

    ⚖️ Know your rights as a worker

    ℹ️
    General principles based on international labor law. Rights vary by country. Consult a local labor lawyer or NGO for advice specific to your situation.
    📄
    Right to a written contract

    You have the right to receive a written employment contract in a language you understand before work begins.

    🪪
    Right to keep your own documents

    No employer has the legal right to hold your passport or national ID. Demand their return at any time.

    💰
    Right to not pay recruitment fees

    Under ILO standards and many national laws, workers should not pay fees to get a job.

    🚪
    Right to leave employment

    You cannot be legally bound to work against your will. If you feel you cannot leave, contact an NGO or labor authority immediately.

    🏥
    Right to safe conditions

    You are entitled to safe working conditions and health protections regardless of your immigration status.

    Technical Principles

    Client-Side Processing

    All linguistic analysis runs in the browser. No text is sent to any server. User input never leaves the device.

    No Data Collection

    LinguaShield collects no personal data, browsing history, or usage analytics in this prototype.

    Weighted Lexicon Engine

    8 named signal categories with pattern matching and confidence scoring — not black-box AI.

    Consent-First Design

    Every data-sharing feature (community submission, evidence upload) requires explicit informed consent before proceeding.

    Responsible AI & Human Rights

    LinguaShield is built on the principle that technology serving vulnerable communities must be held to the highest ethical standard.

    Our Commitments
    "We will not build tools that identify victims, accuse individuals, determine guilt, or enable surveillance — regardless of the intent behind any request to do so."
    No facial recognitionNo victim identificationNo scrapingNo accusationsPrivacy by designConsent-firstEducational only

    The System Will NEVER

    • Identify victims or survivors
    • Identify or accuse traffickers
    • Determine criminal guilt
    • Provide legal advice
    • Replace emergency services
    • Expose personal data
    • Perform facial recognition
    • Track or surveil individuals

    The System ALWAYS

    • Labels AI output as advisory only
    • Requires consent before data sharing
    • Anonymizes all community submissions
    • Applies human review notices
    • Displays educational disclaimers
    • Recommends professional consultation
    • Preserves dignity in storytelling
    • Makes privacy controls visible

    UN Guiding Principles Alignment

    Designed against the UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights — particularly Pillar 1 (duty to protect) and Pillar 3 (access to remedy). Human rights due diligence is built into the design, not retrofitted.

    Survivor-Centered Design

    No gratuitous depictions of harm. Dignity-first storytelling. Anonymized case reconstructions with explicit labeling. Trauma-informed language throughout. Survivors are agents, not victims.

    Known Limitations — Honestly Disclosed

    False positives are possible. Non-English analysis has lower accuracy. Cultural context is incompletely captured. The system is not forensic. All results require human judgment. These limitations are not hidden — they are featured.

    Privacy by Design (7 Principles)

    Proactive, not reactive. Privacy as default. Privacy embedded in design. Full functionality retained. End-to-end security. Visibility and transparency. Respect for user privacy — all seven applied throughout.
